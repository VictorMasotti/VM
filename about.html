ABOUT
Born in 2006 in Rimini, Italy
Lives and works in London, United Kingdom

Victor Masotti’s practice is a contemporary take on the hard edge minimalism movement from the late 1950s and early 1960s, predominantly based around canvases and sculptures. He assembles plastic pieces together, by first crafting the internal structure, over which the pieces are placed onto at different heights, enabling unexpected light reflections and colour combinations. His works blur the lines between the flat plane and the three-dimensionality aspect, where even canvases and occasionally drawings behave and act like their counterparts. Grasping inspiration from a diverse range of sources, from design to art history, fashion and beyond, Masotti has coined a unique visual language, notable for its endless quest to express joy and continuously evolving state.
